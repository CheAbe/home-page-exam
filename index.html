About Kamakura

Kamakura is a city with a rich history, and its significance in Japan goes back to the Kamakura period (1185–1333), which marked the rise of the samurai and the establishment of Japan's first military government, known as the shogunate.

Kamakura is a serene city steeped in history and natural beauty, located just a short train ride from Tokyo. Known for its breathtaking temples, ancient shrines, and the iconic Great Buddha (Daibutsu), Kamakura is a must-see destination for those seeking peace and cultural immersion in Japan.

Whether you’re walking through the bamboo groves, visiting the many Buddhist temples, or enjoying the nearby beaches, Kamakura offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Top Kamakura Attractions

The Great Buddha

Standing 13.35 meters tall, the Great Buddha is one of Japan's most iconic statues.

Hasedera Temple

This temple is famous for its beautiful gardens and large Kannon statue.

Tsurugaoka Hachimangu Shrine

One of the most important religious sites in Kamakura, surrounded by cherry trees.

Hokokuji Temple

Famous for its tranquil bamboo groves and Zen gardens.

Yuigahama Beach

Relax by the sea and enjoy breathtaking views of Kamakura's coastline.
